Article 4 — Risk Disclosure for Venous Blood Collection and On-Site Screening

Venous blood collection is a routine medical-related procedure with good overall safety, but the following discomforts or risks may still occur:

  1. Common reactions: pain at the puncture site, bruising, subcutaneous hematoma, slight bleeding, brief dizziness.
  2. Uncommon situations: needle phobia, blood phobia, or dizziness or brief fainting caused by a vasovagal reflex.
  3. Rare situations: infection at the puncture site, nerve injury, or allergic reaction related to disinfectant or consumables.
  4. Persons with abnormal coagulation function or taking anticoagulant medication may experience persistent bleeding or a larger-area hematoma.
  5. Due to factors such as vascular conditions, on-site cooperation, and physical state, multiple puncture attempts may be required, and collection may not be possible.
  6. Due to reasons such as insufficient sample volume, labeling anomalies, sample contamination, clotting, hemolysis, breakage, abnormal storage or transport, or laboratory rejection, testing may fail or be delayed, or re-collection may be required.

You undertake that you have truthfully disclosed your health condition and any situation that may affect the safety of blood collection or sample handling, including but not limited to: a history of needle/blood phobia, coagulation disease, use of anticoagulant medication, severe anemia, current fever or acute infection, recent surgery, recent blood donation, pregnancy or lactation, a history of severe allergies, and other physical discomfort.

On-site medical personnel have the right to refuse, suspend, or terminate blood collection based on your physical condition, past medical history, medication, on-site performance, blood-collection risk, or other professional judgment. You agree to cooperate with on-site arrangements; if harm or adverse consequences result from your concealment, misreporting, or non-cooperation, you shall bear the corresponding responsibility in accordance with the law.
